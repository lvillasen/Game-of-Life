The Game of Life was invented by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. It is an example of a cellular automaton.

The rules of the game are the following:

  1. Any living cell with fewer than two living neighbours dies.

  2. Any living cell with two or three living neighbours remains alive.

  3. Any living cell with more than three living neighbours dies.

  4. And dead cell with exactly three alive neighbours revives.
